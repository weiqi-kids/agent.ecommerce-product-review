## 1. Problem Root Causes (問題成因)

### 1.1 Dry Skin vs Dehydrated Skin

**Dry Skin (乾性皮膚):**
- Skin type lack of oil (sebum)
- Genetic, environmental factors, aging, medications

**Dehydrated Skin (缺水皮膚):**
- Skin condition lack of water
- Can affect any skin type (even oily skin)
- Environmental factors, diet, lifestyle

Sources: [Cleveland Clinic Dry Skin](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/16940-dry-skin), [Healthline Dehydrated Skin](https://www.healthline.com/health/dehydrated-skin)

### 1.2 Damaged Skin Barrier

**Signs:**
- Dryness, flaking
- Itchiness, rough texture
- Redness, inflammation
- Increased sensitivity
- Acne breakouts

**Causes:**
- Over-exfoliation
- Harsh cleansers
- Environmental aggressors (pollution, UV)
- Low humidity
- Hot showers

Sources: [Cleveland Clinic Skin Barrier](https://health.clevelandclinic.org/skin-barrier), [US Dermatology](https://www.usdermatologypartners.com/blog/damaged-skin-barrier-signs/)

## 2. Solution Comparisons (解決方法比較)

### 2.1 Hydrator vs Moisturizer

**Hydrators:**
- Add water to skin
- Key ingredients: Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, aloe vera
- Lightweight, water-based
- Best for: Dehydrated skin, oily skin

**Moisturizers:**
- Lock in moisture
- Key ingredients: Ceramides, fatty acids, oils
- Richer, oil-based
- Best for: Dry skin, mature skin

Sources: [Kate Somerville](https://www.katesomerville.com/blogs/news/hydrator-vs-moisturizer-whats-the-difference), [Healthline](https://www.healthline.com/health/beauty-skin-care/hydration-moisture)

### 2.2 Emollient vs Humectant vs Occlusive

**Humectants (吸濕劑):**
- Draw water into skin
- Examples: Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, aloe vera

**Emollients (潤膚劑):**
- Fill in cracks between skin cells
- Examples: Ceramides, squalane, shea butter

**Occlusives (封閉劑):**
- Create barrier to prevent water loss
- Examples: Petrolatum, dimethicone, lanolin

Source: [CeraVe Emollient Guide](https://www.cerave.com/skin-smarts/skincare-tips-advice/emollient-vs-humectant-vs-occlusive)

### 2.3 Product Comparisons

**CeraVe vs Vanicream:**

## 4. Technical Principles (技術原理)

### 4.1 Key Ingredients

**Hyaluronic Acid:**
- Holds up to 1000x its weight in water
- Deep hydration
- Reduces fine lines

Sources: [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10078143/), [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/articles/22915-hyaluronic-acid)

**Ceramides:**
- Lipids that make up 50% of skin's outer layer
- Repair and strengthen skin barrier
- Retain moisture

Sources: [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/ics.12972), [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12553851/)

**Niacinamide (Vitamin B3):**
- Reduces redness, inflammation
- Improves skin texture
- Minimizes pores

Sources: [CeraVe](https://www.cerave.com/ingredients-in-cerave/niacinamide), [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16209160/)

1. Toner
2. Essence
3. Serum
4. Moisturizer

Rule: Thin to thick

Sources: [The Stripe](https://thestripe.com/correct-order-apply-skincare-products/), [Copenhagen Fashion Summit](https://www.copenhagenfashionsummit.com/which-comes-first-toner-or-serum-expert-layering-tips/)

### Morning vs Night Moisturizer

**Day Moisturizer:**
- Protection-focused
- Lightweight, often includes SPF
- Antioxidants (Vitamin C)

**Night Moisturizer:**
- Repair-focused
- Richer, thicker texture
- Retinol, peptides, niacinamide

Sources: [L'Oréal Paris](https://www.lorealparisusa.com/beauty-magazine/skin-care/skin-care-concerns/daytime-vs-nighttime-moisturizer), [Charlotte Tilbury](https://www.charlottetilbury.com/us/secrets/day-cream-vs-night-moisturiser)

### Winter Dry Skin Prevention

**Tips:**
1. Moisturize immediately after washing
2. Limit showers to 5-10 minutes, use warm (not hot) water
3. Use humidifier (set to ~60%)
4. Protect face, use lip balm
5. Don't forget sunscreen

Sources: [Harvard Health](https://www.health.harvard.edu/womens-health/what-to-do-about-dry-skin-in-winter), [Mass General Brigham](https://www.massgeneralbrigham.org/en/about/newsroom/articles/how-to-keep-skin-hydrated-in-winter)

## Climate & Environmental Factors (氣候與環境因素)

### Humidity Impact on Skin

**Low Humidity:**
- Reduces stratum corneum water content
- Decreases skin elasticity
- Increases roughness

**High Humidity:**
- Naturally hydrates skin
- May cause oil overproduction, clogged pores

**Optimal Indoor Humidity:** 40-60%

Sources: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27306376/), [Pour Moi](https://pourmoiskincare.com/blogs/beautyforecast/the-impact-of-humidity-on-your-skin)
