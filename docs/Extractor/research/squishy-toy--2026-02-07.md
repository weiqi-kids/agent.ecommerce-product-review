#### ADHD and Fidgeting Research

**Scientific Findings**:
- Research found that **fidgeting improved cognitive performance** in children with ADHD, with similar benefits observed in adults
- A quantitative analysis revealed that **participants exhibited significantly more fidgeting during correct trials** compared to incorrect ones (p<0.001)
- **Fidgeting increased during later task trials**, suggesting it may aid arousal and improve sustained attention
- Participants with **lower reaction time variability** (better sustained attention) showed significantly increased fidgeting, supporting the compensatory mechanism hypothesis

**Mixed Evidence on Fidget Tools**:
- While intrinsic fidgeting (natural body movements) benefits people with ADHD, the effectiveness of commercial fidget devices remains unclear
- **Fidget spinners had an overall detrimental effect on academic performance**, regardless of ADHD symptomatology
- However, one study found **immediate and large increases in on-task behavior** when fidget spinner intervention was implemented

**Research Gaps**:
- The use of fidgets as a universal educational support lacks a strong scientific evidence base
- Notable gap in studies focusing on adults with ADHD
- Current literature examining fidgets' effect on student behavior has significant limitations

**Sources**: [OT4ADHD Fidgets and ADHD](https://ot4adhd.com/2023/05/19/fidgets-and-adhd-a-focus-tool-or-a-toy/), [CHADD Fidget Toys and ADHD](https://chadd.org/adhd-weekly/fidget-toys-and-adhd-still-paying-attention/), [PMC Fidgeting in ADHD Research](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11246969/)

#### Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD)

**What Are Fidgets for SPD**:
- Fidgets are small handheld objects designed to help individuals relieve stress, anxiety, or promote focus and concentration
- They provide sensory experience through tactile feedback, helping to divert excess energy and improve attention

**How Fidgets Help Children with SPD**:
- **Focus and Concentration**: Fidgets enhance focus by channeling excess energy through tactile feedback and sensory experience
- **Sensory Input**: Offer safe, non-disruptive outlet for tactile, auditory, or visual stimulation
- **Motor Skills**: Help develop fine motor skills by working small hand and finger muscles, boosting hand-eye coordination, dexterity, and strength
- **Calming Effect**: Sensory fidgets can be calming and help with focus for individuals with SPD, Autism, and ADHD

**Important Note**: Squirming and fidgeting are common symptoms of hyposensitivity in children with SPD. Under-sensitive kids might be fidgety and unable to sit still.

**Individualized Approach**: Each child has unique sensory needs, so not all fidgets work the same for each child. Multiple fidgets may need to be tried to find the best match.

**Sources**: [SPD Parent Support Fidgets](https://sensoryprocessingdisorderparentsupport.com/sensory-fidgets/), [TheraPlay4Kids 5 Types of Fidget Toys](https://theraplay4kids.com/new-blog-78/2019/7/14/5-types-of-fidget-toys-to-match-your-childs-sensory-processing-needs)

### Solution Method Comparison

#### Squishies vs. Stress Balls

**Material & Design Differences**:
- Both made of polyurethane, but differ in properties
- **PU foam stress balls**: Firmer, recover shape more quickly when squeezed
- **PU squishy balls**: Softer, take longer to regain original shape, slower rebound

**Purpose & Effectiveness**:
- **Stretchy balls**: Designed to encourage play and imagination
- **Stress balls**: Specifically made to reduce stress and anxiety
- Squishies are an "offspring" of stress balls, coming in numerous sizes and shapes (ice cream cones, cupcakes, animals, fruits)

**Scientific Evidence**:
- Studies suggest stress balls work to reduce stress because **squeezing improves the nervous system** and **decreases stress hormones**
- The **pressure activates nerves in hands** that pass signals to the limbic region of the brain (responsible for feelings and emotions)
- However, **effectiveness varies among individuals** and research on effectiveness is mixed

**Specific Use Cases**:
- **PU squishy balls**: For people who want slow rebound and softer feel; popular in retail toy markets, therapy markets; often used as sensory toys for children with autism or ADHD
- **Traditional stress balls**: More effective for targeted stress relief with quicker rebound

**Limitations**:
- May not address all aspects of stress or anxiety
- May not effectively address complex or multifaceted sources of stress
- Unlikely to provide sufficient relief for severe anxiety disorders

**Safety Concerns**:
- Some squishies have raised concerns about **chemical off-gassing** and **strong odors** that can cause headaches
- Less commonly an issue with traditional stress balls

**Sources**: [McGill OSS Squishies](https://www.mcgill.ca/oss/article/health/squeezing-science-out-squishies), [Healthline Stress Balls](https://www.healthline.com/health/stress/do-stress-balls-work), [YoYa Toys Stretchy vs Stress Ball](https://yoyatoys.com/stretchy-ball-vs-stress-ball-whats-the-difference/)

#### Pop-It Fidgets

**Research Findings**:
- A study evaluated effects on listening comprehension ability of elementary students
- In dental anxiety study, groups using pop-its showed **significant reductions in both pulse rate and anxiety scores** compared to control groups
- Combination of pop-it with tell-show-do technique produced the most notable decrease in anxiety indicators

**Limited Evidence**:
- Researchers note that fidget toys are marketed as "universal classroom support," but **evidence basis remains controversial** and largely reliant on theory
- Few peer-reviewed experimental studies exist

**Practical Considerations**:
- Pop-its, along with fidget spinners and light-up discs, **can be misused and cause classroom chaos**
- Researchers and teachers agree to **avoid the most distracting fidgets** (those that make noise or encourage throwing)
- Though research is ongoing, therapists' practical experience suggests fidget toys can be helpful for emotional and cognitive support

**Sources**: [ResearchGate Pop-Its Effects Study](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/369931071_The_Effects_of_Pop-Its_on_The_Listening_Comprehension_Ability_of_Elementary_Students), [Edutopia Do Fidgets Help](https://www.edutopia.org/article/do-fidgets-help-students-focus/)

### Safety and Risks

#### Squishy/Gel Fidget Toys Safety Concerns

**Skin Irritation and Chemical Burns**:
- Consumer Reports investigation found some squishy fidget toys can **break open** and contents may **irritate kids' skin or cause chemical burns**
- Parents reported sticky gel left kids with **red, irritated, or even peeling skin**
- Gel inside NeeDoh Original Groovy Glob had **pH level of 2** (same as lemon juice or vinegar)
- Safety experts say this acidity level could pose **risk of chemical burns**, especially on children's delicate skin

**Durability Issues**:
- Many parents reported toys "popped within an hour" or "broke within two hours"

**Microwave Danger**:
- Consumer Reports found some toys **exploded after just 15 seconds** in microwave, reaching temperatures **above 200 degrees**

**Safety Recommendations**: If toy breaks on child's skin, wash off right away and throw out the toy

**Sources**: [WISH-TV Consumer Reports](https://www.wishtv.com/news/fidget-toys-skin-irritation/), [Yahoo Consumer Reports](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/consumer-reports-squishy-toy-contents-115013297.html)

#### Fidget Spinner Safety (2026)

**Safety Concerns**:
- Choking incidents involving children up to age 14 reported
- 10-year-old girl required surgery after swallowing small component
- Inside spinners: small metal bearings can become loose and pop out
- U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and European safety authorities issued warnings

**Effectiveness Research**:
- Scientific evaluations **did not confirm effectiveness** in improving focus

**Overall**: Fidget spinners and other self-regulatory occupational therapy toys **have yet to be subjected to rigorous scientific research**, and alleged benefits remain scientifically unfounded
